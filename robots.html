Our Robots

Since 2006, Team 1912 has designed and built innovative robots to compete in the FIRST Robotics Competition. Each robot represents countless hours of dedication, engineering excellence, and the collaborative spirit of our team.

Drivetrain

Mechanisms

2-stage elevator, articulated end effector, and coral indexing using four beam-break sensors

Pulsar (2024)

Game: CRESCENDO

Drivetrain

Mk4i L3 swerve drive

Mechanisms

Lightweight under-bumper intake, on-the-fly shooter with auto-aim, fast amp cycling system, and dual-stage climber for both low and high chain climbs

Features

Multiple autonomous routines, LED driver indicators, and versatile scoring from all field positions

Jympmie (2023)

Game: CHARGED UP

Drivetrain

Swerve Drive Specialties Mk4i with REV Neo/REV SparkMax and CTRElectronics CANcoder

Mechanisms

ThriftyBot elevator kit driven by a REV through-shaft single-speed gearbox with two NEOs; custom intake deployed using a chain-linked virtual-4-bar linkage

Zuko (2022)

Game: RAPID REACT

Drivetrain

Swerve Drive Specialties Mk4 with REV Neo/REV SparkMax and CTRElectronics CANcoder

Mechanisms

Custom intake, indexer, shooter, and a climber capable of reaching the high bar

12 Parsecs (2021)

Game: Infinite Recharge At Home

Modifications

Reused the 2020 robot with new code for trajectory-following autonomous using USDigital encoders and KauaiLabs NavX gyro

12 Parsecs (2020)

Game: INFINITE RECHARGE

Built on the kit-of-parts chassis with a roller intake, hopper, indexer, elevator, and a shooter built on the Greyt turret. Competed in the semi-finals at the Arkansas Regional before the season was halted due to the pandemic.

Artemis (2019)

Game: Destination: Deep Space

Built on a kit-of-parts chassis; excelled at cargo-station filling and consistent mid- or high-level climbing.

Q*Burn (2018)

Game: FIRST POWER UP

Faced challenges during the season, serving as a valuable learning experience.

Captain Amelia (2017)

Game: FIRST STEAMWORKS

Named to align with the steampunk style of the game.

Templar (2016)

Game: FIRST STRONGHOLD

Low-profile robot on a 6-wheel custom chassis with 8-inch pneumatic wheels designed to navigate game defenses.

Fury (2015)

Game: RECYCLE RUSH

Equipped with a large pneumatic cylinder to lift tote stacks and recycling bins; utilized mecanum wheels for side-to-side movement to align with stacks.

Apache (2014)

Game: Aerial Assist

Catapult powered by custom elastic bands made from heavy-duty speargun tubing, launched using a cam release to project large exercise balls into goals.

Hammerhead (2013)

Game: Ultimate Ascent

Double-pneumatic-wheel frisbee launcher with dual PID loops fed by optical rotary encoders for shooter speed control; included a pair of pneumatic cylinders to hang on the bottom rung of the pyramid.

Thermite (2012)

Game: REBOUND RUMBLE

Spinning wheel launcher for foam balls, roller intake, surgical-tubing conveyor, and pneumatic lift for ball intake and indexing.
